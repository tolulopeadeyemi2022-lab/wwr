Having no credit history can feel almost as limiting as having bad credit. A secured credit card for building credit is usually the fastest, cheapest way to open that door. You put down a refundable deposit, use the card responsibly, and your on-time payments start showing up on your credit reports within a month or two.

## What Is a Secured Credit Card?
A secured credit card works like a regular credit card, except you back it with a cash deposit that usually becomes your credit limit. The deposit protects the issuer, which is why approval is easier than with unsecured cards even if you have no credit file at all.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
* **Maxing out the card:** High credit utilization can hurt your score even if you pay in full every month.
* **Making only minimum payments:** This leaves a balance that accrues high interest rates.
* **Applying for multiple cards at once:** Each application triggers hard inquiries that can temporarily lower your score.
* **Closing your account too soon:** Keeping accounts open builds your average credit history length.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does a secured credit card deposit cost?
Most require a minimum deposit between $200 and $300, though some options allow deposits as low as $49.

### Can I get my deposit back?
Yes. Most issuers refund your deposit once you close the account in good standing or upgrade to an unsecured card.

## Key Takeaways
A secured credit card reports your payment history just like a regular card, using your refundable deposit to simplify approval. Focus on zero annual fees, affordable deposits, and full bureau reporting.
