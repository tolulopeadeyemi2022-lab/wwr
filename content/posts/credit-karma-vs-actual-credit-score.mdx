You check Credit Karma before a big purchase and your score looks solid. Then the lender pulls your credit and hands you a completely different number. That gap confuses almost everyone the first time it happens, but it comes down to a simple fact: Credit Karma and the credit report your lender pulls are not measuring the exact same thing.

## What Credit Karma Actually Shows You
Credit Karma provides free access to two credit scores pulled from TransUnion and Equifax using the VantageScore 3.0 model. It updates frequently—sometimes daily—giving you a real look at your credit report without hurting your score. 

### VantageScore 3.0 Factors
* **Payment history:** Whether you have paid on time, carrying the most weight by far.
* **Credit utilization and balances:** How much of your available credit you are using.
* **Credit age:** How long your accounts have been open.
* **Credit mix and experience:** A healthy variety of accounts.
* **New credit and inquiries:** How often you have applied for credit recently.

### Credit Score Ranges
Both VantageScore and most FICO models use the standard 300 to 850 range:

* **800 to 850:** Exceptional
* **740 to 799:** Very Good
* **670 to 739:** Good
* **580 to 669:** Fair
* **300 to 579:** Poor

## What Your "Actual" Credit Score Means
When people refer to their actual credit score, they usually mean the FICO score utilized by mortgage lenders, auto lenders, and credit card issuers. Because FICO and VantageScore use different formulas, and lenders may pull from Experian instead of TransUnion or Equifax, your numbers will naturally vary.

## Where the Numbers Split
* **The scoring model:** Credit Karma uses VantageScore 3.0 while most lenders use FICO.
* **The bureau:** Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion each maintain separate files.
* **The timing:** Credit Karma updates within days, whereas a lender's snapshot depends on the exact day of application.

## How to Check Your Actual FICO Score
* Check your credit card statement or mobile app, as many major issuers include a free FICO score.
* Ask your lender directly when you formally apply for a loan.
* Use platforms like myFICO.com for industry-specific versions.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Credit Karma accurate?
Yes, it accurately reflects your VantageScore 3.0 from TransUnion and Equifax, though it is not the exact model most lenders use for final decisions.

### Does checking Credit Karma hurt my credit score?
No. Checking your score on Credit Karma is a soft inquiry that has zero impact on your credit rating.

## Key Takeaways
Credit Karma uses VantageScore 3.0 from TransUnion and Equifax, while lenders typically rely on FICO. Variations are driven by different scoring models, credit bureaus, and reporting timelines. Focus on the underlying habits—like on-time payments and low utilization—that improve every version of your score.

## What To Do Next
Check your VantageScore on Credit Karma today, compare it with a FICO score from your banking app if available, and pull your full reports from AnnualCreditReport.com if you notice any major discrepancies.
