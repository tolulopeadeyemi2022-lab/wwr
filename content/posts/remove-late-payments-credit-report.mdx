One missed due date years ago can still be dragging your score down today. If you're wondering how to remove late payments from your credit report, the honest answer is that it depends on whether the mark is accurate.

If it's an error, you have real legal leverage. If it's accurate, you still have options, just different ones. 

## Why a Single Late Payment Hits So Hard
Payment history makes up the biggest chunk of your credit score. One 30 day late mark can drop a strong score by 60 to 100 points, and lenders remember that for years. 

Fortunately, late payments aren't permanent and they aren't always accurate. Some were reported by mistake, while others can be resolved through persistence and the right letter.

## Step 1: Pull All Three Credit Reports
Before you dispute anything, head to AnnualCreditReport.com and request your reports from Experian, Equifax, and TransUnion. Creditors don't always report to every bureau, so you need to check all three to find every error.

## Step 2: Confirm Whether the Late Payment Is Wrong
Compare the reported date against your own bank statements or payment confirmations. Common errors include payments processed late by the bank, mixed files with similar names, or unapplied deferments. If your records show you paid on time, you have a legitimate dispute.

## Step 3: File a Dispute With the Credit Bureau
Under the Fair Credit Reporting Act, you can dispute inaccurate information online, by mail, or by phone. 
* Once submitted, the bureau forwards your dispute to the creditor.
* The creditor has roughly 30 days to investigate and respond.
* If the creditor cannot verify the accuracy, the bureau must remove or correct the mark.

## Step 4: Send a Goodwill Letter for Accurate Marks
If the late payment is accurate, a dispute won't work. Instead, send a polite goodwill letter asking the creditor to remove the mark as a courtesy. Goodwill letters work best when the slip was a one-time occurrence, your account history is otherwise spotless, and you take accountability without making excuses.

## Step 5: Know When It Falls Off Automatically
Under the FCRA, most late payments can only stay on your credit report for seven years from the date of the missed payment. If an item is older than seven years and still listed, it is a straightforward error worth disputing immediately.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
* **Disputing everything at once** with vague blanket claims, which can get flagged as frivolous.
* **Skipping documentation** like bank statements or payment confirmations.
* **Giving up after one rejection** instead of escalating or providing extra evidence.
* **Paying a credit repair company** for services you can easily handle yourself for free.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I remove an accurate late payment?
Not through a dispute. An accurate late payment can only be addressed via a goodwill letter or by waiting for it to age off after seven years.

### How long does a bureau have to investigate?
Generally 30 days, though it can extend to 45 days if you submit additional information during the process.

## Key Takeaways
Inaccurate late payments can be disputed and removed under federal law, while accurate ones can sometimes be resolved using goodwill letters or patience as they age off after seven years.
