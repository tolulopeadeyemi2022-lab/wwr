A single wrong entry on your credit report can lower your score and cost you money. Knowing how to dispute credit report errors is one of the most useful financial skills you can have, and it doesn't require a lawyer or a fee.

## Why Credit Report Errors Matter
Lenders, landlords, and employers use your credit report to make financial decisions. Studies from the Federal Trade Commission show that a meaningful share of consumers have at least one error on their report, ranging from simple typos to accounts belonging to someone else entirely.

## Common Credit Report Errors to Watch For
* **Accounts that don't belong to you** due to identity theft or mixed credit files.
* **Late payments** reported on accounts you paid on time.
* **Incorrect balances** or outdated information.
* **Duplicate accounts** appearing twice under slightly different names.

## Step-by-Step Dispute Process

| Step | Action | Details |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Step 1** | Pull All Three Reports | Get free copies from Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion via AnnualCreditReport.com. |
| **Step 2** | Review Every Line | Go account by account checking balances, dates, and account statuses. |
| **Step 3** | Gather Documentation | Collect bank statements, payoff letters, or police reports to back up your claim. |
| **Step 4** | File Bureau Disputes | Submit online or via certified mail with supporting evidence. |
| **Step 5** | Dispute With Furnisher | Send a direct dispute to the bank or company that originally reported the item. |
| **Step 6** | Track the Timeline | Bureaus generally have 30 days to investigate and respond. |

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
* **Disputing everything at once** with vague blanket claims, which can get flagged as frivolous.
* **Skipping documentation** like bank statements or payment confirmations.
* **Giving up after one round** when roughly one in five disputes require a second attempt.
* **Paying credit repair companies** for services you can easily handle yourself for free.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does a credit report dispute take?
Bureaus generally have 30 days to complete an investigation, though it can extend to 45 days if you submit additional information.

### Can disputing an error hurt my credit score?
No. Filing a dispute does not affect your credit score at all.

## Key Takeaways
Credit report errors are common and fully correctable. Pull all three reports, document your case clearly, and pursue disputes with both the credit bureau and the original furnisher.
