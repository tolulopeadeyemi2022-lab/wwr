Credit scores affect everything from loan interest rates to apartment approvals, yet well-meaning relatives and questionable videos keep outdated myths in circulation. A 100-point difference in your score can mean tens of thousands of dollars in extra interest over a mortgage's life, making it essential to separate fact from fiction.

## Myth 1: Checking Your Own Credit Score Hurts It
Checking your own score through a bank app or free annual report is a soft inquiry. Soft inquiries never affect your score, whereas hard inquiries happen when lenders evaluate applications for new financing.

## Myth 2: Closing Old Credit Cards Improves Your Score
Closing an old account reduces your total available credit, which can spike your credit utilization ratio and shorten your average account age. 

## Myth 3: You Need to Carry a Balance to Build Credit
Scoring models do not care if you carry a balance from month to month. They track on-time payments and your statement balance utilization, meaning you can pay in full every month and build excellent credit while paying zero interest.

## Top Credit Myths vs. Facts Compared

| Common Myth | The Actual Fact |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Checking your credit hurts it** | Soft inquiries from checking your own score have zero impact. |
| **Marriage merges scores** | Spouses keep separate files unless they share joint accounts. |
| **High income equals high score** | Income is not part of a credit score calculation. |
| **Paid collections vanish instantly** | Paid collections can remain on your report for up to seven years. |

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
* **Applying for multiple credit cards at once**, stacking hard inquiries that temporarily chip away at your score.
* **Closing multiple old cards** right before applying for a major loan, which spikes your credit utilization ratio.
* **Assuming credit repair companies** can legally erase accurate negative marks for a high fee.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does checking my credit score every week hurt it?
No. Checking your own score through a soft inquiry never affects it, no matter how often you check.

### Will paying off my car loan early boost my score?
It may cause a small, temporary shift due to changes in your credit mix, but it will not cause a dramatic score jump.

## Key Takeaways
Payment history and credit utilization drive the majority of your score. Focus on boring, repeatable habits like paying on time, keeping balances low, and checking your report for errors yourself.
